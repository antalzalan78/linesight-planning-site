Services

Three concrete ways to start

Each engagement has a clear scope, a concrete deliverable and a business decision it enables, so you know what you get before we start.

01 3–12 months

Interim Production Planning Support

Typical problem
Your planner leaves, goes on leave or the workload doubles, and schedules start slipping within weeks.
What you get
Day-to-day production schedules kept running from week one, plus a documented planning process and structured handover to the permanent team.
Data needed
Access to your ERP/APS, demand plan and line master data.
The decision it enables
Keep delivery commitments reliable while you hire or reorganise, without a rushed recruitment.
02 2–4 weeks

Capacity & Headcount Planning Scan

Typical problem
You suspect next year's volumes will not fit within your available lines and shifts, but there is no quantified capacity view behind the concern.
What you get
A capacity model per line and product group, shift & headcount scenarios side by side, and a written recommendation.
Data needed
12–18 months of demand history plus forecast, line speeds/OEE, shift calendar.
The decision it enables
Decide on shift patterns, hiring, overtime or investment with numbers on the table, before the peak hits.
03 4–8 weeks

Backlog Recovery & Weekly Planning Stabilisation

Typical problem
Orders are late, the backlog keeps growing, and every week is firefighting instead of planning.
What you get
A prioritised recovery plan with a realistic catch-up date, and a weekly planning cycle (meeting, rules, KPIs) your team keeps running.
Data needed
Open order book, line capacities and current schedule adherence.
The decision it enables
Set more reliable customer delivery dates, and decide what to expedite, outsource or renegotiate.
